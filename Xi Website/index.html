THE ARCHIVE

Extinct Concepts

The following rights and experiences were once considered fundamental to human existence. They are presented here as historical artefacts.

EXTINCT
Est. 1991 — Declared lost: 2031

Anonymous Browsing

The belief that a person could navigate the internet without their identity being permanently attached to each action. Browser fingerprinting, device IDs, and real time identity verification rendered this concept functionally obsolete before its formal end was declared.

0% survival probability
EXTINCT
Est. forever — Declared lost: 2034

Untracked Movement

The ability to move through public space without being continuously logged. Real time facial recognition, gait analysis, and connected sensor networks eliminated this concept entirely. Your location, route, duration, and social connections in public are now permanent records.

0% survival probability
CRITICAL
Est. origin of consciousness — Status: under threat

Private Thought

Neural interface technology, developed under the premise of medical necessity, began harvesting emotional response data, attention patterns, and pre verbal cognitive signals. As adoption became near universal, the boundary between thought and data dissolved. Not yet extinct. Barely.

4% survival probability
ENDANGERED
Est. 2014 (EU) — Status: critically weakened

The Right to Be Forgotten

Once a legally protected right allowing individuals to request deletion of their personal data. Successive policy rollbacks under pressure from commercial and governmental interests have reduced this right to a formality. Requests are technically accepted and rarely actioned.

12% survival probability
EXTINCT
Est. 1990s — Declared lost: 2029

Opt Out Consent

The theoretical option to refuse data collection while still participating in modern society. As surveillance became embedded in employment, healthcare, transport, and housing systems, opting out became indistinguishable from opting out of society itself. The option was quietly removed.

0% survival probability
EXTINCT
Est. origin of language — Declared lost: 2033

Private Communication

The assumption that a conversation between two people was known only to those two people. End to end encryption was systematically legislated against under national security frameworks across most jurisdictions. All digital communication is now retained, searchable, and cross referenced.

0% survival probability
